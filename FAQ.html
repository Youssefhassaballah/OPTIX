Optix Dental Loupes - Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Optix loupes different from others on the market?

Optix loupes are engineered for maximum clarity, comfort, and balance. We use advanced optics and premium materials to reduce fatigue and enhance precision — all with a sleek, ergonomic design that fits naturally into your workflow.

Are the loupes through-the-lens (TTL) or flip-up?

Optix offers both TTL and flip-up models. TTL is ideal for maximum clarity and stability, while flip-up models offer flexibility and easy adjustment — depending on your personal preference and workflow.

What magnification levels are available?

We currently offer magnification levels from 2.5x up to 8x, with 10x coming soon. This wide range allows clinicians to choose the ideal level of detail for different types of procedures.

Are the loupes custom-fitted?

Optix loupes are not fully custom-made, but each set is carefully adjusted and calibrated to match your interpupillary distance (IPD), working distance, and posture — ensuring optimal comfort and vision alignment.

What kind of lighting system is included?

Optix loupes can be paired with a lightweight, high-intensity LED headlight, featuring adjustable brightness, even light distribution, and a compact battery pack — all designed to keep you focused without distractions.

How much do the loupes weigh?

Depending on the lens and lighting system, total weight ranges between 400g to 600g. Thanks to perfect weight distribution, most users find them much lighter and more comfortable than expected — even during long procedures.

Can I use Optix loupes with my prescription lenses?

Yes. We offer prescription integration for TTL models, and can adapt frame configurations to suit your visual requirements without compromising clarity or balance.

Are they comfortable to wear with a face shield or mask?

Absolutely. Optix loupes are designed with low-profile frames that fit comfortably under standard dental PPE, including masks and face shields, without interference.

What is the battery life of the headlight system?

Each battery lasts up to 6 hours of continuous use. Optix kits come with two batteries, giving you a full 12-hour workday without downtime.

What’s the warranty and support policy?

Optix loupes come with a 1-year warranty and lifetime free maintenance and technical support. We’re committed to supporting you long after your first use.
